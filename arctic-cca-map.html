★ IDEAL ARCTIC CCA DESIGN NOTE
Required Combat Radius: 1,000–1,100 nm
An aircraft operating from any FOL in the NWS network (Inuvik, Cambridge Bay, Resolute Bay, Iqaluit, Thule) must reach all Arctic flashpoints:
• Inuvik → Bering Strait: ~850 nm
• Resolute Bay → Fram Strait: ~1,100 nm
• Thule → Franz Josef Land: ~1,050 nm
• Cambridge Bay → North Pole: ~1,100 nm
• Iqaluit → Davis Strait: ~300 nm

On-Station Endurance: 2–4 hrs
Track-quality ISR (sensor dwell, multi-pass correlation) requires ≥2 hrs on station. Kinematic engagement (intercept + re-attack) needs fuel margin for A/B maneuver + egress = 3–4 hrs ideal.

Total Mission Profile:
• Transit out: 1.5–2 hrs (M 0.7–0.85)
• On-station: 2–4 hrs (loiter + engage)
• Transit back: 1.5–2 hrs
• Reserve: 30–45 min (Arctic divert)
• Total: 5.5–8.75 hrs unrefuelled

Combat Load Requirement:
• Internal: 2× AIM-260 / 2× SDB-II (min)
• ISR: EO/IR + AESA radar + ELINT
• Comms: MADL/Link-16 + BLOS SATCOM
• EW: internal RF jammer + RWR

Key Insight: No current CCA achieves all requirements. The 1,100nm ring shows the minimum reach needed. Aircraft falling short (Fury 700nm, Valkyrie 750nm) require AAR support, limiting surge and persistence. The ideal Arctic CCA needs clean-sheet range ≥ 1,000nm with 3+ hrs on station.
